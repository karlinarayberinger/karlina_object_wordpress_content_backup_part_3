Note: a sperm is significantly cheaper to produce than is an egg. Also, one organism can typically produce functional sperm across its entire adult lifespan (even in old age). ovum: a haploid which only female humans produce (in organs known as ovaries (and ovaries have the same evolutionary origin as testes)). Note: an ovum is significantly more costly to produce than is a sperm. Presumably for that reason, female humans historically have a limited window of fertility (in terms of montly ovulation cycles and in terms of age range of fertility). chromosome: a sub-cellular structure which is transferred from one organism to another via the propagation of gametes which contains the 4-digit encoded blueprint for implementing some specific set of features in the organism that genetic code is designed to instantiate. genotype: the complete collection of genetic instructions required to instantiate exactly one organism. phenotype: the implementation of a particular genotype as a living organism. zygote: a diploid which is the synthesis of two complementary haploids (particularly one ovum and one sperm).
