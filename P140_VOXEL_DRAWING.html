VOXEL_DRAWING

VOXEL: a unit of three-dimensional Cartesian space (which is a static region bound by three axes which intersect at perpendicular angles at the origin point of the voxel grid (and each of the three axes (i.e. the horizontal line, the vertical line, and the depth line) span in infinite directions both ways).

By “static”, I mean to imply that purely abstract space is inert (i.e. does not morph (which means that the voxels are timelessly rigid structures stacked together as cubic boxes with no space between adjacent boxes)).

horizontal_line : x-axis :: vertical_line : y-axis :: depth_line : z-axis.

A single voxel occupies exactly one unit of the x-axis, exactly one unit of the y-axis, and exactly one unit of the z-axis.

A single voxel is a finite microcosm of the entire Cartesian three-dimensional graph.

Each of the eight corners of the voxel is the intersection of x-axis, y-axis, and z-axis lines at perpendicular angles.
