BODILY_AUTONOMY
After walking around with my shirt off and my pants rolled up to the knees in the flats near Dublin/Pleasanton BART station, I started feeling much better than I did earlier today. I felt that I had room to breathe more freely, move more freely, and relax in a more spacious and naturalistic environment than the claustrophobic urban center where I usually hang out. I saw minnows swimming in a school in a shallow creek and Canadian geese standing in a large green field. The breeze and warm sunlight felt good against my pallid skin. After walking around for a bit, I decided to place an order for some Protab Cold Water Hash Sativa tablets from the most local cannabis store for approximately $35: Garden of Eden on the border of Hayward and Castro Valley. I am going to walk back to the Dublin/Pleasanton BART station, board the train, and get off at the Castro Valley BART station since that station is closest to the cannabis store (and so that I do not have to walk through the congested and dilapidated parts like I normally have up until now). Yes, air quality is a concern I can no longer deny having. Mission Boulevard tends to be very smoggy up close because it is a main artery for (mostly petroleum powered) vehicles through the East Bay region. I will write more after I acquire the loot. See you then!
