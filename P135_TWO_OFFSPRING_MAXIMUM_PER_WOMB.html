TWO_OFFSPRING_MAXIMUM_PER_WOMB
As I write this note, I sit in the Dublin/Pleasanton BART parking garage; charging my Android phone and Lenovo (Kubuntu) laptop. Without disclosing the details of my recent experience while traveling from Castro Valley to Dublin/Pleasanton, I feel compelled to do a national president's job: define the terms of what I believe is a progressive means to mitigate unsustainable human population growth (within one "first world" country). After a citizen gives birth to two offspring, that citizen shall be permanently sterilized as soon as possible after its second offspring is born. Such a sterilization procedure is arguably necessary because (a) Planet Earth already contains more human organisms than it can sustain long-term (which means that the human species will almost certainly go extinct within the next 500 years if the total human population size does not shrink to a more sustainable number), (b) the current birth-to-death ratio of human organisms is currently too high for Planet Earth can sustain long-term (which means that the human species will almost certainly go extinct within the next 500 years if the birth-to-death ratio of human organisms does not reach a range of equilibrium), (c) and making it physically impossible for exactly one human womb to give birth to more than exactly two offspring prevents more than one inseminating partner from using that womb to "cheat" by using it that womb to yield more than two offspring total prevents citizens from using serial monogamy as an excuse to produce more than their fair share of offspring. Unlike many Christian types (people who endorse the biblical commandment to "be fruitful and multiple" despite the ecological, social, and personal costs), I believe that human reproduction is not the same thing as being a prolific artist. That is because, unlike pieces of intellectual property, human organisms are fragile and needy creatures which each cost a significant amount of resources to properly nurture and maintain. Also, I endorse abortions for first-trimester human fetuses for pregnant humans who want to opt out of incubating that fetus to its birth point.
