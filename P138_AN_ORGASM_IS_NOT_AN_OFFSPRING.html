Before I continue unraveling my “doubly secure” plan to help the human species quickly (and cheaply) mitigate and permanently resolve the crisis of overpopulation (which I think is the root cause of systemic poverty, violent territorial disputes, and delayed scientific and cultural progress), I would like to share some “technical definitions” which I personally find interesting as a biology nerd to think about…

haploid: a specialized cell within a multicellular organism which contains only half the genome of its constituent multicellular organism.

diploid: a cell within either a single-celled organism (i.e. prokaryote) or else a multi-cellular organism (i.e. eukaryote) which contains the entire genome of its constituent organism.

gamete: a haploid designed to transfer half the genome of its originating multicellular organism to exactly one complementary haploid such that a synthesis of exactly two half-genomes of the same species can be produced in an offspring of that species.

sperm: a haploid which only male humans produce (in organs known as testes (and testes have the same evolutionary origin as ovaries)). Note: a sperm is significantly cheaper to produce than is an egg. Also, one organism can typically produce functional sperm across its entire adult lifespan (even in old age).

ovum: a haploid which only female humans produce (in organs known as ovaries (and ovaries have the same evolutionary origin as testes)). Note: an ovum is significantly more costly to produce than is a sperm. Furthermore, once an ovum is fertilized by a sperm, the incubating body invests 100% more resources in carrying that zygote to term than the sperm-dealing counterpart body does. Presumably for that reason, female humans historically have a limited window of fertility (in terms of monthly ovulation cycles and in terms of age range of fertility).

chromosome: a sub-cellular structure which is transferred from one organism to another via the propagation of gametes which contains the 4-digit encoded blueprint for implementing some specific set of features in the organism that genetic code is designed to instantiate.

genotype: the complete collection of genetic instructions required to instantiate exactly one organism.

phenotype: the implementation of a particular genotype as a living organism.

zygote: a diploid which is the synthesis of two complementary haploids (particularly one ovum and one sperm).
